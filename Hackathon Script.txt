## Hackathon Demonstration Script

## Intro Slide Video

(Narrative): Institutions are ready for DeFi, but they are currently paralyzed by the risk of non-compliant liquidity. We solve this with the Agentic Compliance Bridge—a privacy-preserving 'Compliance Firewall' that ensures only cryptographically proven actors enter the pool.

The Dynamic Compliance Firewall skill acts as an automated gatekeeper, vaulting funds from non-compliant users and releasing them only after ZKPs are securely ferried via Chainlink CCIP to guarantee a legally compliant cross-chain environment. This dual-layer system uses an off-chain Agentic Brain to audit private bank APIs and on-chain Guards to secure an Escrow Vault—locking 10 ETH on the source chain and triggering atomic release only when a ZKP mathematically proves the user meets our 100 ETH wealth and US residency requirements. This is what we will walk through in today's demo

The Chainlink Runtime Environment enables our Agentic Compliance Bridge to operate off-chain, handling private logic like ZKP generation and bank API audits that cannot be performed on-chain, while Chainlink Functions verifies real-world data (Mock Bank) and pulls attestations into our smart contracts to ensure only verified actors enter the pool.

Phase 2:
COMMAND 3- The Dual-ZKP Trigger (Attestation & Bridging)
This command tells the Agentic Compliance Bridge to audit the Mock Bank's credentials, originate the Zero Knowledge Proof via the & broadcast to CCIP.

We used the Circom 2.0 compiler to define our private logic, requiring the user to have over 100 ETH and US residency, while ensuring the bank never leaks sensitive PII. 

The Agentic Bridge API in Terminal 2 utilizes SnarkJS as a proving engine to generate a Zero-Knowledge Proof off-chain, so raw data never touches the public ledger. This proof is then ferried via Chainlink CCIP to Arbitrum, where our Solidity verifier contract sees only a cryptographic "Yes" or "No" to verify compliance and unlock the firewall.

## Ending Slide - Demonstrated Capabilities

(Narrative): We built our Agentic Compliance Bridge on a Chainlink Customized Runtime Environment (CRE). This off‑chain compute layer hosts our bridge agent, enabling it to ping Chainlink Oracles for data,  generate Zero‑Knowledge proofs, and audit bank data via Chainlink Functions—effectively connecting TradFi's privacy requirements with DeFi's need for transparency.

Traditional bridges move money and assets, which is slow and vulnerable to hacks. Our Agentic Bridge moves Compliance Proofs, allowing for Instant Atomic Settlement of assets that are already secured on the destination chain.

Our Hackathon Submission enables a legally compliant, low-fee LP environment for the Clarity Act era. By unifying ZK-privacy with Chainlink’s secure infrastructure, we’ve replaced the friction of manual compliance with a cryptographic gateway—transforming a regulatory hurdle into a mathematically-guaranteed reality.
