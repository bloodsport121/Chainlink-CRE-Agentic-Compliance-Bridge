## Hackathon Demonstration Script

## Intro Slide Video

Narrative: Institutions want to use Defi, but they cant risk interacting with non compliant liquidity. Our Agentic Compliance Bridge solves this by deploying a custom-built skill called Dynamic Compliance Firewall, where Zero Knowledge Proofs and CCIP are used to create a privacy-preserving 'Compliance Firewall' that will only release a transaction from Escrow into the compliant defi environment if it is cryptographically proven to be a compliant transaction. 

The Chainlink Customized Runtime Environment (CRE) powers our Agentic Compliance Bridge (via API in Terminal 2) by enabling it to operate as an off-chain agent. This allows the bridge to handle private, complex logic such as ZKP generation and bank API audits—that cannot be performed directly on the blockchain.

Chainlink Functions is the primary product powered by the CRE. It allows the Compliance Bridge to reach out to the real world (The Mock Bank), verify data, and pull that attestation back into our smart contracts.

The Agentic Compliance Bridge operates as a business' Compliance Firewall. It is made of two primary parts: Off-Chain Agentic Brain (Terminal 2 API) and the On-Chain Guards (Tenderly Smart Contracts).

It's job is to:
1. connect a traditional bank with REST APIs (something blockchain can't do) and convert certain compliance details into Zero-Knowledge Proofs.

2. The Agentic Compliance Bridge requires two simple credentials that must be proven in single cryptographic bundle to be allowed to enter into our compliant DeFi environment. For the purpose of this demonstration the user must satisfy a 100 ETH Minimum Wealth rule AND the USA Residency rule. These are the hypothetical requirements to be legally compliant in a local jurisdiction. 

3. It controls the InstitutionalEscrow contract. It is the only entity authorized to open the escrow vault after a successful cross-chain compliance check.

COMMAND 3- The Dual-ZKP Trigger (Attestation & Bridging)
This command tells the Agentic Compliance Bridge to audit the Mock Bank's credentials, originate the Zero Knowledge Proof via the & broadcast to CCIP.

We used the Circom 2.0 compiler to write the compliance.circom file, defining the ZKP's private logic (User has > 100 ETH and US residency). Then, we utilized SnarkJS as the proving engine; the Agentic Bridge in Terminal 2 uses this library to generate a Groth16 ZK-Proof, which is ferried cross chain via Chainlink CCIP to be verified by our Solidity contract on Arbitrum.
