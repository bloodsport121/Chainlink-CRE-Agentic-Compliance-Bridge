## Hackathon Demonstration Script

## Intro Slide Video

(Narrative): Institutions are ready for DeFi, but they are currently paralyzed by the risk of non-compliant liquidity. We solve this with the Agentic Compliance Bridge—a privacy-preserving 'Compliance Firewall' that ensures only cryptographically proven actors enter the pool.

The Dynamic Compliance Firewall acts as an automated institutional gatekeeper that vaults funds from non-compliant users in a secure escrow, releasing them only after privacy-preserving Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) are securely ferried via Chainlink CCIP to mathematically guarantee a legally compliant cross-chain environment.

The Chainlink Customized Runtime Environment (CRE) enables our Agentic Compliance Bridge (via API in Terminal 2) to operate as an off-chain agent. This allows the bridge to handle private, complex logic such as ZKP generation and bank API audits—that cannot be performed directly on the blockchain.

Chainlink Functions is the primary product powered by the CRE. It allows the Compliance Bridge to reach out to the real world (Mock Bank), verify data, and pull that attestation back into our smart contracts.

Our Agentic Compliance Bridge functions as a dual-layer "Compliance Firewall," utilizing an off-chain Agentic Brain to audit private bank APIs and on-chain Guards to secure a local Escrow Vault. Instead of a risky asset bridge, we lock 10 ETH on the source chain and only trigger an atomic release once a ZKP mathematically proves the user meets our 100 ETH wealth and US residency requirements.

Phase 1:

10 ETH is sent from the user to the Agentic Compliance Bridge. It is not yet flagged as compliant so the funds are locked into an escrow holding vault.

COMMAND 2-Bottom Terminal 3
It shows our recipient wallet address inside the compliant DeFi ecosystem as well as the empty wallet balance of 0 ETH

Phase 2:
COMMAND 3- The Dual-ZKP Trigger (Attestation & Bridging)
This command tells the Agentic Compliance Bridge to audit the Mock Bank's credentials, originate the Zero Knowledge Proof via the & broadcast to CCIP.

We used the Circom 2.0 compiler to define our private logic, requiring the user to have over 100 ETH and US residency, while ensuring the bank never leaks sensitive PII. 

The Agentic Bridge API in Terminal 2 utilizes SnarkJS as a proving engine to generate a Zero-Knowledge Proof off-chain, so raw data never touches the public ledger. This proof is then ferried via Chainlink CCIP to Arbitrum, where our Solidity verifier contract sees only a cryptographic "Yes" or "No" to verify compliance and unlock the firewall.
